# Communication Prompts

Prompts for announcements, release notes, FAQs, LinkedIn posts, and handling hard feedback.

---

## Feature Announcement Draft

**Use case:** Draft a clear launch post for users.
**Input needed:** Feature value, target users, key benefits, availability details.

---

Write a product announcement with:
- Headline
- Problem we are solving
- What is new
- Who it is for
- What users can do now
- CTA

Tone: clear, specific, credible. Avoid hype words.

---

**Tips:**
- Lead with user outcome, not internal model details.
- Add one concrete before/after example.

---

## Release Notes Transformer

**Use case:** Convert technical change logs into user-facing release notes.
**Input needed:** Engineering release notes, bug fixes, improvements, known limitations.

---

Transform this release input into user-friendly notes.

Format:
- New
- Improved
- Fixed
- Known limitations
- What to try next

Keep language simple and action-oriented.

---

**Tips:**
- Highlight behavior changes users will notice immediately.
- Include known limitations to preempt avoidable support tickets.

---

## Internal FAQ Generator

**Use case:** Equip support, sales, and CS with consistent answers.
**Input needed:** Feature details, pricing/packaging, constraints, policy boundaries.

---

Create an internal FAQ for cross-functional teams.

Need at least 20 Q&A pairs covering:
- Value proposition
- Setup/use
- Limitations
- Pricing and access
- Security/trust
- Troubleshooting

Answers should be short, accurate, and non-technical where possible.

---

**Tips:**
- Include "what not to promise" answers for customer-facing teams.
- Update FAQ after first two weeks of support tickets.

---

## LinkedIn Post Angle Generator

**Use case:** Create social post drafts that attract the right audience.
**Input needed:** Product lesson, proof point, target audience, call to action.

---

Generate 5 LinkedIn post angles from this launch/lesson.

For each angle provide:
- Hook line
- Core narrative (3–5 bullets)
- Credibility signal
- CTA

Audience: PMs and founders shipping AI products.
No clickbait.

---

**Tips:**
- Use one strong proof point per post (metric, screenshot, or lesson).
- End with a specific question to boost high-quality comments.

---

## Tough Feedback Response Draft

**Use case:** Respond to critical user feedback without defensiveness.
**Input needed:** User complaint, context, current product behavior, plan.

---

Draft a response to this critical feedback.

Must include:
- Acknowledgement of issue
- What is true now
- What we are doing next
- Timeline expectation
- Optional workaround

Tone: accountable, calm, transparent.

---

**Tips:**
- Never over-promise fixes you cannot ship quickly.
- If unresolved, provide a clear update checkpoint date.
